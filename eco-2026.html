ECF & Surplus Transfer
  • ECF (2019) — Based on Bimal Jalan Committee; reviewed every 5 years
  • Under Section 47, RBI Act 1934
  • Realized Equity = Capital + Reserve Fund + Contingency Fund + ADF
  • CRB = Contingent Risk Buffer (monetary/financial stability risks)
  • FY25 surplus transferred: ₹2.69 Lakh Crore
Forex Reserves Components
  • FCA — USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, JPY
  • Gold Reserves — Now $102.3 bn (≈15% of forex reserves, up from 7% decade ago)
  • SDR — Interest-bearing IMF reserve asset
  • Reserve Tranche Position (RTP) — Quota minus IMF's holdings; no strict conditions
One State One RRB
  • 4th phase amalgamation → 28 RRBs (down from 43) in 26 states+2 UTs
  • Earlier phases based on Dr. Vyas Committee recommendations
  • Genesis: 1975, Narasimham Working Group
  • Ownership: GoI 50% | State 15% | Sponsor Bank 35%
  • Regulated by RBI under Banking Regulation Act 1949; supervised by NABARD
  • Must allocate 75% of ANBC to PSL; CRAR 9%
Small Finance Banks
  • Licensed under Section 22(1), Banking Regulation Act 1949
  • Announced in Union Budget 2014-15; registered as public limited company
  • Min paid-up capital: ₹200 crore
  • AU SFB → first SFB to get in-principle approval for Universal Bank transition
  • Criteria: 5 yr scheduled status, stock listed, net worth ₹1,000 cr, GNPA ≤3%, NNPA ≤1%
  • New PSL rule (FY26): Total PSL reduced to 60% ANBC (from 75%)
SARFAESI Act 2002
  • Objective: Recovery of NPAs (loan overdue >90 days = NPA)
  • Methods: Securitisation | Asset Reconstruction | Court-free enforcement (Sec 13)
  • ARC = buys NPAs to clean up bank balance sheets; registered with RBI under SARFAESI
  • Grievance: Borrowers → DRT (Recovery of Debts & Bankruptcy Act 1993)
  • Sec 13(8) contradiction with Rules 8&9 highlighted by Supreme Court
RB-IOS 2021
One Nation One Ombudsman — Merged 3 schemes (Banking 2006, NBFC 2018, Digital Txn 2019). Co-op banks now covered. Award: ₹20 lakh + ₹1 lakh (harassment)
SRO for NBFCs
RBI granted SRO status to FIDC. SROs set up as Sec 8 (not-for-profit) companies. No entity >10% paid-up share. 1/3rd Board must be independent.
New RBI Initiatives
ULI = Digital Public Infrastructure for credit delivery. CBDC Retail Sandbox for fintech innovation. Tokenisation of CDs on blockchain.
PSL Priority Sector Lending
PSL Categories
  • Agriculture | MSMEs | Export Credit | Education | Housing | Social Infrastructure | Renewable Energy
  • Applicable to: Commercial Banks (incl. RRBs, SFBs, LABs) & Primary (Urban) Co-op Banks

Co-lending norms
Min 10% share each. PSL status claimable by each lender. Uniform NPA classification — if one tags NPA, all must.

03 Financial Market & Payments
SEBI
  • Set up 1988; statutory body by SEBI Act 1992
  • Board: 1 Govt-nominated Chairman + 2 MoF + 1 RBI + 5 Govt-nominated (≥3 full-time)
  • New: SWAGAT-FI Framework for trusted FPIs & FVCIs
  • REITs reclassified as equity (enables MF investment)
  • AIF: Reg. under SEBI AIF Regs 2012. Cat I (startups) | Cat II (PE/Debt) | Cat III (Hedge Funds)
UPI & Digital Payments
  • UPI launched 2016 by NPCI; built on IMPS infrastructure
  • Per-transaction limit: ₹5 lakh; daily limit (P2M): ₹10 lakh
  • Namibia = first African country to adopt UPI (2025)
  • UPI active in: Bhutan, France, Mauritius, Nepal, Singapore, Sri Lanka, UAE
  • NPCI = RBI + IBA initiative; not-for-profit; also runs RuPay, FASTag (NETC), NACH
  • NIPL (NPCI subsidiary, 2020) deploys RuPay & UPI outside India
GIFT City IFSC
  • India's first & only IFSC; SEZ since 2015; Gandhinagar, Gujarat
  • Regulated by IFSCA (est. 2020, IFSCA Act 2019)
  • New: FCSS (Foreign Currency Settlement System) — local USD settlement via CCIL IFSC
  • GFI Ranking: 46th globally; FinTech: 45th
Bond Terms
  • Bond Price ↑ = Yield ↓ (inverse relationship)
  • AT1 Bonds = Perpetual bonds; no maturity; call option; meet capital adequacy
  • Transition Bonds = Sub-category of green debt; for hard-to-abate sectors (steel, cement)
  • India's S&P Rating: Upgraded to BBB (from BBB-); investment grade range: BBB- to AAA
04 External Sector
FDI Key Facts
  • Governed by: FEMA 1999 | Consolidated FDI Policy 2020 | FEMA (NDI) Rules 2019
  • 90% of FDI now via automatic route
  • Net FDI fell 96% in FY25 vs FY24 (despite gross FDI rise)
  • Top sectors FY25: Service (19%) | Computer software (16%) | Trading (8%)
  • Top countries: Singapore (30%) | Mauritius (17%) | USA (11%)
  • Prohibited: Lottery, Gambling, Chit funds, Nidhi, Atomic Energy, Railway ops
Remittances
  • India #1 recipient globally; USD 118.7 bn in 2023-24
  • Top source: USA (27.7%) → UAE → UK → Saudi Arabia → Singapore
  • Top recipient states: Maharashtra (20.5%) → Kerala → Tamil Nadu
  • = 47% of India's merchandise trade deficit; >10% of gross current account inflows
  • US Remittance Tax: 3.5% excise tax proposed (effective Jan 2026)
  • LRS: ₹250,000/FY for resident individuals; not for HUFs/trusts
Rupee Internationalization
  • SRVA mechanism (July 2022): Invoice & settle trade in INR
  • Vostro = foreign bank's account in domestic bank (in INR)
  • Nostro = domestic bank's account in foreign bank (in foreign currency)
  • REER = NEER × (Domestic PI / Foreign PI); lower REER = undervalued = export boost
  • India: Current account fully convertible; Capital account partially convertible
  • Other steps: ACU INR inclusion, Masala bonds, Bilateral swap agreements
India-UK FTA (2025)
  • ≈99% of Indian exports get zero-duty to UK; India cuts levies on 90% of British products
  • Eases mobility for Indian professionals (contractual, business visitors, ICTs)
  • UK businesses can participate in government procurement
  • DCC (Double Contributions Convention) also agreed = social security in one country at a time; up to 3 years
India-EFTA TEPA (Oct 2025)
  • EFTA = Iceland + Liechtenstein + Norway + Switzerland (India's largest partner in EFTA)
  • First FTA with binding investment + job commitment: $100 bn investment, 1 mn direct jobs in 15 years
  • EFTA offers 100% access on non-agricultural products
  • IT/business services/education exports stimulated; MRAs for nursing, CA, architecture
Export Promotion Mission
  • Outlay: ₹25,060 crore | Timeline: FY2026–FY2031
  • Sub-schemes: Niryat Protsahan (financial) + Niryat Disha (non-financial)
  • Implementing Agency: DGFT
  • India's total exports FY25: $824.9 bn (Merch $437.4 bn + Services $387.5 bn — historic high)
  • CGSE: ₹20,000 crore collateral-free credit; 100% guarantee by NCGTC

06 Agriculture & Allied Sector
PM Dhan Dhaanya Krishi Yojana (PMDDKY)
  • Announced: Union Budget 2025-26; inspired by NITI Aayog's Aspirational Districts
  • Consolidates 36 schemes across 11 ministries
  • Coverage: 100 underperforming districts (min 1 per state)
  • Target: 1.7 crore farmers; ₹24,000 cr/yr for 6 years (total ₹1.44 lakh cr)
  • Budget: 40% subsidies | 30% infrastructure | 20% loans | 10% training
  • Goal: Double farmer incomes by 2030; post-harvest losses <5%
Mission for Aatmanirbharta in Pulses
  • Target: Self-sufficiency by Dec 2027
  • Outlay: ₹11,440 cr; Duration: 6 years
  • Focus crops: Tur/Arhar, Urad, Masoor
  • 100% MSP procurement for 4 years via NAFED & NCCF under PM-AASHA
  • India = largest producer, consumer & importer of pulses
  • Top state: MP (22.11%) → Maharashtra → Rajasthan
PMMSY (Fisheries)
  • Launched 2020; Dept of Fisheries; extended to FY26
  • India = 2nd largest fish producer; 8% of global production
  • India = largest inland capture fish producer globally
  • Fisheries = 1.12% national GVA; 7.26% agricultural GVA
  • Inland fish = 75% of total; listed in State List
  • Achievement: Per capita consumption 5-6 kg → 12-13 kg

09 Infrastructure
Maritime & Ports
  • India: 12 major ports (13th under construction — Vadhavan, Palghar, MH); ~200 non-major
  • 95% trading by volume; 70% by value = maritime
  • India = 16th largest maritime nation; 3rd in ship recycling by tonnage
  • LPI (World Bank): 44th (2018) → 22nd (2023)
  • Sagarmala 2015: MoPSW; 5 components (Port modernisation, connectivity, port-linked industrialisation, coastal community development, coastal shipping)
  • Sagarmala 2.0 (2025): Focus on shipbuilding & port modernization
  • NSAC chaired by Minister of Shipping; SCSC by Cabinet Secretary
  • Indian Ports Act 2025 replaces 1908 act; MSDC & SMBs get statutory recognition
New Shipping Acts 2025
  • Merchant Shipping Act 2025: Mandatory registration all vessels; expands vessel definition
  • Carriage of Goods by Sea 2025: Aligned with Hague-Visby Rules 1924
  • Coastal Shipping Act 2025: Regulates coastal trade; "coastal waters" = territorial waters + EEZ
  • Bills of Lading Act 2025: Replaces 1856 act
  • Vizhinjam Port: India's first deep-water container transshipment port; Kerala; PPP landlord model; 10 nautical miles from int'l shipping route
Logistics & Asset Monetisation
  • NLP 2022: Complement to PM GatiShakti NMP (2021); 57+ ministries integrated; 7 engines of NMP
  • Target: Top 25 LPI by 2030; India at rank 38 (2023)
  • ULIP: 30+ digital systems; 160 crore+ transactions
  • Asset Monetisation: Does NOT mean disinvestment; TOT / DBFOT / InvITs / REITs models
  • NMP (National Monetisation Pipeline): ~₹6 lakh crore over 2022-25
Road Safety
  • NRSB: Section 215B, Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act 2019
  • Cashless Road Accident Scheme 2025: ₹1.5 lakh; 7 days; MoRTH; State Health Agency pays within 10 days
  • Good Samaritan: Section 134A, MVA 1988 (added by 2019 amendment)
  • Bharat NCAP 2.0: 5 areas; voluntary; CIRT Pune issues rating
