  • Majuli: World's largest riverine island (in Brahmaputra, Assam)
  • China dam at Nyingchi City near 'Great Bend'; 2,000m drop over 50 km — massive hydro potential
  • Right-bank tributaries: Kameng, Manas, Beki, Raidak, Teesta
  • Left-bank tributaries: Lohit, Dibang, Subansiri, Kopili, Dhansiri

Physical Geography — Concepts

A

Volcanoes

Types of Volcanoes
  • Shield Volcano: Broad, gently sloping; thin fluid lava; less explosive (e.g., Hayli Gubbi, Ethiopia)
  • Stratovolcano (Composite): Steep, cone-shaped; explosive (e.g., Lewotobi, Indonesia; Mt. Etna, Sicily)
  • Cinder Cone: Smallest; steep; formed from ejected lava fragments
  • Lava Dome: Viscous lava piling near vent
  • Complex/Compound Volcano: Two or more vents/domes (e.g., Taal, Philippines)
  • India's Volcanoes: Barren Island (only active; South Asia's only active volcano) | Narcondam Island (dormant) | Mud Volcano, Baratang, A&N (geological only)
  • Mud Volcano: Erupts mud + water + methane; no molten lava; found near natural gas areas
  • Mt. Etna: Europe's largest active volcano; Sicily; UNESCO WHS since 2013
Barren Island = South Asia's only active volcano; India's only mud volcano = Baratang
Melting Glaciers → Volcanic Eruptions
  • Ice exerts pressure on magma chambers; melting ice reduces pressure → gases & magma expand → explosive eruptions
  • In Iceland: Volcanic eruption rates were 30-50× higher during major deglaciation periods
  • West Antarctica has ~100 volcanoes buried under ice
B

Earthquakes

India's Revised Seismic Zones (2025)
  • BIS released revised Earthquake Design Code (IS 1893) 2025 — new Zone VI added
  • Entire Himalayan region → Zone VI (highest risk)
  • 61% of India's landmass and 75% of population under moderate-to-high seismic hazard
  • Previous: 4 zones (II–V); Now: 5 zones (II–VI)
  • Method: Probabilistic Seismic Hazard Assessment (PSHA)
Himalayan Fault Systems
  • MCT (Main Central Thrust): Greater Himalayas vs Lesser Himalayas
  • MBT (Main Boundary Thrust): Lesser Himalayas vs Shiwaliks
  • MFT/HFF (Main Frontal Thrust / Himalayan Frontal Fault): Shiwaliks vs Indo-Gangetic Plains
Key Earthquake Facts
  • India = 7th most earthquake-prone country
  • 1950 Great Assam Earthquake: M 8.6; epicentre Mishmi Hills, Arunachal-Tibet border; combined strike-slip + thrusting
  • Human-induced Quakes: Mining, groundwater pumping (Delhi-NCR), dam impoundment (Koyna 1967 = M 6.3), fracking
C

Monsoon & Weather

Southwest Monsoon
  • Contributes 70%+ of annual rainfall (June–September)
  • Normal onset over Kerala: June 1 | Covers entire India by ~July 15
  • IMD uses: Rainfall amount + wind speed/direction + OLR to declare onset
  • LPA (Long Period Average): Average June-September rainfall over 50 years; benchmark for forecasting
Key Monsoon Drivers
  • ENSO: El Niño (warm E. Pacific) → reduced rainfall; La Niña → stronger monsoon
  • IOD: Positive IOD (Arabian Sea warmer than Bay of Bengal) → favourable for monsoon
  • Mascarene High: High-pressure near Mascarene Islands (S. Indian Ocean); drives west coast rains
  • Somali Jet: Low-level inter-hemispheric wind; strong jet = stronger monsoon
  • MJO (Madden-Julian Oscillation): Eastward moving 30-60 day cycle; identified by Madden & Julian in 1971
  • Atmospheric Memory: Atmosphere stores water vapour; bistability — if raining, rain persists
ENSO, IOD, MJO definitions and impact on monsoon — key for Prelims
Bharat Forecasting System (BFS)
  • Developed by IITM, Pune; 6 km × 6 km resolution — highest in world (earlier 12 km)
  • Enabled by new supercomputer 'Arka'
  • IMD established: 1875; HQ moves: Calcutta → Shimla → Pune → New Delhi
  • Mission Mausam: ₹2,000 crore; target 60 weather radars + 15 wind profilers + 15 radiosondes by 2026
Cyclones
  • Formation: SST > 26.5°C (depth 50m), unstable atmosphere, Coriolis force, low wind shear
  • Coriolis: Anticlockwise in NH; Clockwise in SH; weak within 5° of equator
  • Names: Hurricane (Atlantic/E. Pacific) | Typhoon (W. Pacific) | Cyclone (Indian Ocean)
  • NIO Cyclone Season: Pre-monsoon (Mar-May) & Post-monsoon (Oct-Dec)
  • Super Typhoon: Sustained winds > 185 kmph / 100 knots
  • Saffir-Simpson Scale: Category 1-5; Category 3+ = major hurricanes
  • Colour Alerts: Green → Yellow (be aware) → Orange (be prepared) → Red (take action)
Cloudbursts & GLOFs
  • Cloudburst: ≥10 cm rain in 1 hour over ~10 km × 10 km area; Orographic lift → delayed release
  • Mini-cloudburst: ~5 cm in 1 hour over 20-30 sq km; harder to predict
  • GLOF: Abrupt discharge from glacial lake; India has 7,500 glacial lakes
  • Major GLOFs: South Lhonak (2023, Sikkim) | Chorabari (2013, Kedarnath)
  • UN declared 2025 as International Year of Glaciers' Preservation
  • Yala Glacier (Nepal): Shrunk 66% since 1970s; first glacier funeral: Okjokull Glacier, Iceland (2019)

Maratha Military Landscapes

  • India's FIRST UNESCO World Heritage Site; Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, Maharashtra

Archaeological Sites

Keezhadi Excavations
  • Location: Sivaganga district, TN; Vaigai river; Sangam age urban civilization

Khadir Island, Kutch
  • Shell-midden sites near Dholavira reveal human presence 5,000 years before Harappan era

  • Radiocarbon dating: Based on C-14 decay; up to ~60,000 years; developed by Willard Libby (late 1940s)
Zehanpora Excavation (J&K)
  • Baramulla, J&K; Kushan period (~2,000 years old); ancient Buddhist stupas
  • Emperor Ashoka believed to have founded Srinagar; established monasteries in Kashmir

UNESCO & International Heritage

Recent UNESCO Designations
  • Maratha Military Landscapes: India's 44th WHS (47th WHC)
  • Erra Matti Dibbalu: Red sand dune formations on Andhra Pradesh coast
  • Sarnath: Buddhist heritage site; Buddha gave first sermon here
  • Sirpur Archaeological Site: Chhattisgarh; proposed UNESCO nomination
  • Intangible Cultural Heritage: Ramman Festival (Uttarakhand), Jamdani weaving
BBNJ Treaty (High Seas Treaty)
  • Full name: Biodiversity Beyond National Jurisdictions — legally binding under UNCLOS
  • Aims: Curb pollution, limit resource extraction, create protected areas in high seas, equitable benefit-sharing
  • High Seas: Beyond national territorial waters and EEZs; ~64% of ocean area; global commons
  • India: Signed but NOT yet ratified; drafting domestic law (12-member committee; headed by Sanjay Upadhyay)
  • 60+ countries have ratified; enters force 120 days after 60th ratification
  • UNCLOS (1982): Territorial sea = 12 NM (full sovereignty); EEZ = 200 NM (economic rights only)
BBNJ vs UNCLOS; India = signed but not ratified
Mercator Map & Equal Earth
  • Mercator projection: Designed 1569 by Gerardus Mercator; preserves shape & angles but distorts area near poles
  • Africa (30 mn sq km) appears same size as Greenland (14× smaller); Europe appears 3× its actual size vs Africa
  • Equal Earth projection (2018): Preserves relative area accurately
  • African Union endorsed 'Correct the Map' campaign to replace Mercator with alternatives
  • Gall-Peters projection: Also preserves area but elongates continents

Important One-Liners

  • Metre Convention (1875): International treaty establishing BIPM; standardises SI units
  • Yuge Yugeen Bharat Museum: National museum showcasing India's history from ancient to modern
  • Rashtra Prerna Sthal: 65-acre lotus-shaped memorial, Lucknow (Gomti river); statues of Vajpayee, Deen Dayal Upadhyay, Shyama Prasad Mookerjee
  • National Ayurveda Day: Observed on Dhanvantari Jayanti (Dhanteras)
  • Wazwan: Traditional multi-course meal of Kashmiri cuisine
  • Nyoma Air Base: One of world's highest airfields (4,200m); southeastern Ladakh; ~35 km from LAC
  • Sagarmatha Sambaad: Nepal-hosted multilateral dialogue; theme: 'Climate Change, Mountains, and Future of Humanity'
  • Coastline Paradox: Coastline length increases with finer measurement scale; identified by Lewis Fry Richardson; popularised by Benoît Mandelbrot
  • Cherrapunji Eastern Craft Gin: First alcohol label from NE India to be exported; made from rainwater of Sohra & Mawsynram
  • Operation Flood (1970): White Revolution; transformed India's dairy sector; architect: Dr. Verghese Kurien
  • Bekal Fort: Largest & best-preserved fort in Kerala (Kasaragod); built c.1650 by Shivappa Nayaka (Keladi dynasty); constructed from laterite
  • Mud Volcano (Baratang, A&N): India's only mud volcano; erupted after 20 years; erupts mud + methane (no lava)
  • Daojali Hading (Assam): Neolithic site in Dima Hasao; 2,700+ years old; artefacts include polished double-shouldered celts
  • Dandakaranya: Large forested region spanning Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Telangana, AP; drained by Mahanadi & Godavari tributaries; Dandaka forest of Ramayana
