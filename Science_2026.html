⚛️ 3. QUANTUM TECHNOLOGY
📐 Concept: Key Quantum Principles
Superposition: particle in multiple states until measured | Entanglement: two linked particles; measuring one instantly affects other
Interference: particles cross paths; constructive or destructive | Qubit: quantum bit = 0 AND 1 simultaneously (vs classical bit = 0 OR 1)
Quantum Tunnelling: particle passes through energy barrier (e.g., radioactive alpha decay) 
Classical Bit: 0 | 1
Qubit:         0 AND 1 (superposition) → measured → collapses to 0 or 1
Entanglement:  [Qubit A] ←quantum link→ [Qubit B] → instant correlation

🌐 Ordinary Matter & Dark Matter
  • Harvard+Caltech: detected universe's "missing" ordinary matter via Fast Radio Bursts (FRBs)
  • FRBs = electromagnetic radiation bursts from distant galaxies; ms duration; disperse like prism → reveal matter

  • Dark matter = 27% cosmos; invisible (doesn't interact with light); exerts gravity
  • Matter-antimatter: Big Bang should produce equal amounts; universe is almost all matter

⚡ 8. ALTERNATIVE ENERGY
📐 Concept: Nuclear Fission vs Fusion
FISSION: neutron hits heavy atom → splits → chain reaction → energy
  Fuel: Uranium/Plutonium | Waste: long-lived radioactive | Tech: proven (reactors)

FUSION: two light atoms (H isotopes: D+T) slam together → heavier atom → massive energy
  Fuel: Deuterium+Tritium | Waste: only helium+neutrons (short-lived) | Tech: experimental
  Requires: plasma at 150,000,000°C | confined by magnetic field (Tokamak)
  Net Energy Gain (NEG) = critical for commercial viability

☢️ India's 3-Stage Nuclear Programme
  • Stage I – PHWR fueled by natural Uranium → produces Pu (Plutonium)
  • Stage II – Fast Breeder Reactor (FBR); Pu+natural U fuel; Th blanket → U-233; PFBR Kalpakkam
  • Stage III – Th+U-233 fuel; very large scale electricity from India's vast thorium reserves
  • India: world's largest Th reserves; Kerala, Odisha, AP, TN (monazite sands)
  • Uranium enrichment: 3–5% civilian; >20% HEU; ~90% weapons-grade
[Mains GS3]
🔋 Next-Gen Battery Technologies
  • Li-ion – high energy density; lightweight; low self-discharge; no memory effect
  • Flow Batteries – redox reactions; liquid electrolytes; store large amounts; types: Vanadium Redox, Zinc-Bromine, Iron-Salt
  • Solid-State Batteries (SSB) – solid electrolyte; safer (no leakage/fire); 4–6× faster charging; more energy density
  • Sodium-Ion Battery (SIB) – DST developed; charges 80% in 6 min; 3000 cycles; transported at zero voltage (safer); uses Na+ instead of Li+
  • Black Mass Recovery – TDB funded; dual-mode wet+dry recovery of Li, Co, Ni from used batteries
